INTRODUCTION OF THE SOLAR SYSTEM

The Solar System our cosmic neighborhood, is a vast and intricate collection of celestial bodies that revolves around the Sun, the central star of our system. It consists of eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and the Kuiper Belt, all bound together by the Sun’s gravity. The study of the Solar System is not only a journey through the universe but also an exploration of the origins of our own existence, the laws of nature, and the potential for life beyond Earth.

Our Solar System is Consist Of

The Sun and The 8 planets

The Sun

"Old Sol"

The Sun is a massive, glowing sphere of hot plasma that serves as the center of our Solar System. It provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth and governs the movements of the planets through its powerful gravitational pull. The Sun is classified as a G-type main-sequence star (G2V), also known as a yellow dwarf.

🔹G2V (Yellow Dwarf)
🔹~4.6 billion years old
🔹1,392,700 km (109 times Earth's diameter)
🔹828,000 km/h (514,000 mph) Rotation
🔹25 days (at equator), 35 days (at poles): ~59 Earth days)
🔹274 m/s² (28 times stronger than Earth's gravity)
